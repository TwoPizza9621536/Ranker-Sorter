Ranker Sorter

If you can rate/rank chess players by using Elo, then why not characters.

This is yet another ranker that I created using the Elo Rating System, famous in the world of chess to rate the skills of players as a zero-sum game. But this rating system has also been used in other applications like dating apps and love. If love is a zero-sum game than why not the interest or attention of a character or thing that are comparable (or anything that is zero-sum game).

To assume that the interest of a character is a zero-sum game, we need to measure interest based on how much attention they get, and that attention is limited. Which means attention for a character is neither gain nor removed, but only taken away or from other characters to have an interest for that character. In other words when a person shows an interest for new character, their interest is lessen for a preexisting established character.

Frequency Asked Questions

What is the Elo Rating System?

The Elo rating system is a method for calculating the relative skill levels of players in zero-sum games such as chess. It is named after its creator Arpad Elo, a Hungarian-American physics professor.
~ Wikipedia

Are the results accurate and should I take those results seriously?

Slightly yes with a grain of salt. Elo is mainly based on statistics and probability. A rating is just a number just label the probability of liking a character. Probability is just a number that tells how likely a character is being your most favorite which includes you not having that character to be your most favorite.
